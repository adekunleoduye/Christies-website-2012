Case Study 6: Assisting an Art Adviser

An art adviser recommended to her client, a major collector, that he consolidate and upgrade his art storage to facilitate collection management and ensure all pieces are properly preserved. The adviser visited various warehouses, collected proposals, and selected CFASS as the optimal partner.

CFASS arranged for property from 4 different warehouses spread across the eastern coast of the United States to be transferred. Upon arrival at CFASS, each piece was unpacked, inspected, photographed, repacked appropriately for storage, barcoded, and moved into the clients Managed Private Unit. This unit space was surprisingly less than the sum of the client's previous storage spaces because CFASS was able to provide racking and maximize space efficiency. The client enjoyed a smaller storage footprint as well as a higher quality facility -- which appeased his and his adviser's concerns that property would deteriorate over time from dust, moister, and other harmful additives.

One month after signing with CFASS, the adviser's client urgently needed his adviser to examine one of his pictures. CFASS installed the picture in a viewing gallery and provided the adviser with the requested materials – examination table, black light, and work station. With the client on the phone from abroad, the adviser performed the necessary work and conveyed all details to her client. All parties involved were pleased with the service and confident that consolidating and upgrading their art storage with CFASS was the ideal solution.
