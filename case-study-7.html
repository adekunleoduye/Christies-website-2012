Case Study 7: A Foundation Seeking a Long-Term Plan

An artist foundation was seeking support managing their collection and developing a long-term plan. Their property was stored in several non-climate controlled locations. Periodically, the Foundation would sell select works privately or at auction to fund the foundation's operations. The Foundation Director found this plan to be largely ineffective; the condition – and therefore value- of the artworks was being compromised in the low-quality storage, and the organization's finances were unstable. They approached CFASS for assistance.

CFASS first developed a customized pricing plan to match the Foundation's needs. Property was then consolidated at CFASS and secured in a Managed Private Unit. CFASS inspected all property upon receipt and immediately informed the Foundation about condition issues with a number of the artworks. Per the Foundation's request, CFASS managed the restoration process by negotiating rates, scheduling selected restorers, creating the necessary on-site studio space, and moving property as needed. CFASS then worked hand-in-hand with the Foundation to understand its goals and develop a plan that offered long-term financial stability. As part of this plan, CFASS periodically acts as the Foundation's Agent to sell select pieces in the collection through recommended sale channels. Their storage space at CFASS is consolidated accordingly, and they enjoy the comfort of having their property well preserved and a sustainable long-term plan.
