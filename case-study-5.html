Case Study 5: Private Clients with a Growing Collection

A private client with a growing collection was advised by her insurer to move any art that was not exhibited at home into a purpose-built storage facility. The insurer indicated that the ad-hoc basement storage space was no longer safe, and the artworks' condition and value were at risk. The client had a selection of paintings, silver, and antiques sent to CFASS. CFASS created a full inventory of all pieces in its care and had all property secured in a Managed Private Unit. All property details and images are available to the collector online through MyCFASS, and the collector uses this online account management system to reserve viewing galleries when needed. Last year, the client was seeking liquidity and worked with her wealth advisor to use select artworks as collateral against a loan. The collateral was secured at CFASS, and the removal of that property is regulated by CFASS according to the wealth advisor and client's instruction. As the client's collection grows, she rotates her home exhibition and sends more pieces to CFASS expanding her unit size as needed.
